2.6.3 Solution) Boundary conditions constitute the following: . Finite
as x-: ++\(\psi_1(x\rightarrow-\infty) < \infty\)++ . Finite as x:
++\(\psi_3(x\rightarrow\infty) < \infty\)++ . Continuous at
boundaries: ++\(\psi_1(x=a)=\psi_2(x=-a)\)++ and
++\(\psi_2(x=a) = \psi_3(x=a)\)++ . Derivative of the wave
function is continuous at boundaries:
++\(d\psi_1/dx|_{x=-a} = d\psi_2/dx|_{x=-a}\)++ AND
++\(d\psi_2/dx|_{x=a} = d\psi_3/dx|_{x=a}\)++

Applying these conditions to the results from 2.6.2: .
++\(\psi_1(x=-\infty)<\infty\)++ A=0
++\(\psi_1(x) =  Be^{kx}\)++

{empty}2. ++\(\psi_3(x=\infty)<\infty\)++ D=0
++\(\psi_3(x)=Ce^{-kx}\)++

++\(Be^{-ka}=Ce^{-ila} + De^{ila}\)++(eq.1) and
